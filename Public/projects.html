  • Ongoing Grades/GPA Calculator Website

    With my completion of SYSC 2004 "Object Oriented Software Development" at Carleton University, I thought it would be a useful and necessary tool to be able to use my new found knowledge of object oriented programming in Java to make a tool that focuses on helping students determine if they are on the right track as far as grades go in their respective programs. I was able to make use of a GUI, Java classes and related interfaces to implement this project.

    The goal of this project was to be able to provide my peers with a public resource where they can gain an accurate representation of where their grades are at, and what they would need to succeed granted the information they provided was accurate and complete. I left room for estimates to be made so students can see where their overall marks would be at given their marks fall within a certain range.

    The idea behind this project was me seeing that since my first year in university, I have had friends come up to me to ask for assistance with calculating where their overall grades were at in a course. As this didn't seem to be a problem for me where I would basically look into their course outlines to determine the weighting of each assessment(assignments, tests, exams...) and their current grades to determine where they currently stand and what they would need in order to reach their desired grade point. My understanding of Object-Oriented programming both proved to be the most effective method to tackling the problem (as procedural programming usually needs a certain "structure" to its problems) and opened my mind to the idea that this resource can just be made accessible to all students and there would be no need to account for human error(if programmed correctly).

    The final step to this project which I am still yet to implement would be to make use of my newly gained web development knowledge and figure out how to transfer my code to its own separate website (or application if necessary) as there seems to be a learning curve for students outside programming related fields to run the code to its maximum efficiency.
